Techtrends Symposium

The Techtrends Symposium, held on 19th and 20th September 2024, was a remarkable two-day event organized by IEEE JSSATEN Student Branch. Day 1 featured inspiring keynote sessions by distinguished speakers, including Mr. Abhishek Tiwari (Scientist E, CDAC Noida), Dr. John Jose (Associate Professor, IIT Guwahati, and Chief Guest), Mr. Sandeep Agarwal (Director at Emerson Automation Solutions), Mr. Anuj Kumar (Manager at Grid Controller of India Limited), Mrs. Manshi Sharma (Strategic Planning Manager, Emerson), Mr. Gaurav Pathak (Vice Chair, IEEE EdSoc YP Ad Hoc), and Mr. Sachin Kumar (IEEE Young Professional). Day 2 concluded with an engaging industrial visit to CDAC, offering participants a hands-on experience with cutting-edge technological advancements. The event was a perfect blend of learning, networking, and exploration, leaving participants with unforgettable memories and invaluable insights.

Dronecone workshop

The Drone Cone event was an exciting three-day journey into the world of drones, offering participants a comprehensive learning experience from theory to practice. On Day 1, attendees delved into the fundamentals of drone technology, exploring the theoretical aspects and gaining insights into its mechanics and applications. Day 2 focused on hands-on simulation training, where participants practiced and honed their skills in a controlled environment. Finally, Day 3 culminated in a thrilling competition, where participants who attended the workshop on the first two days showcased their expertise and creativity in an engaging challenge. The event successfully combined learning, innovation, and competition, leaving participants with a deeper understanding of drone technology and memorable experiences.

Review

LINE FOLLOWER ROBOT(LFR)

The Line Follower Robot (LFR) event was a two-day workshop held on February 16-17, 2023. It provided participants with a deep dive into the world of robotics, focusing on the design and programming of robots that can autonomously follow a path. Day 1 covered the theoretical aspects of line-following technology, while Day 2 allowed participants to build and program their own robots in a hands-on environment. The event was a perfect blend of learning and innovation, fostering technical skills and problem-solving abilities.

IEEE Day

To celebrate IEEE Day on October 8th, two exciting events were organized. The first was an online Poster Making Competition, where participants showcased their creativity and innovation. The second event was an offline workshop on Circuit Crafts, where attendees gained hands-on experience in building and designing circuits. Both events provided a platform for learning, creativity, and collaboration, making the IEEE Day celebration memorable.
